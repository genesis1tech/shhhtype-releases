# Rewrite Rules By Category
## How to Transform Each Transcript Segment Into Hormozi Copy

Apply the rules for the detected category. Preserve facts from original. Amplify, never fabricate.

---

## UNIVERSAL HORMOZI RULES (Apply to Every Category)

Before applying category-specific rules, enforce these on ALL rewrites:

1. **Active voice. Always.** Find every "is/are/was/were [verb]ed" and rewrite actively.
2. **Kill every adverb.** Delete every -ly word. Find a stronger verb instead.
3. **Short sentences.** If it has a comma, see if it should be two sentences.
4. **Simple words.** 5th-grade reading level. "Use" not "utilize." "Start" not "commence."
5. **Contractions mandatory.** "Don't" not "do not." "Can't" not "cannot."
6. **Specific numbers.** Replace every vague claim with a number if available in original.
7. **Positive framing.** Tell them what TO do, not what not to do (except "How to stay [bad thing]" framework).
8. **No hedging.** Delete "I think," "maybe," "perhaps," "sort of," "kind of."
9. **No filler.** Delete "basically," "essentially," "really," "very," "just," "literally."
10. **Whitespace.** Every sentence or short thought gets its own line. Content must breathe.

## CATEGORY 1: HOOK / OPENER

### Hormozi's Standard: The hook's ONLY job is to make someone stop scrolling. Under 60 characters when possible.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Lead with the punch** — No warm-up, no context, no "Hey everyone"
2. **Use a proven hook type** — Number, contrarian, negative flip, bold declaration, or direct address
3. **Create a curiosity gap** — Reader should think "Wait, what?" or "How?"
4. **Be specific** — "$46.2M" not "millions." "99%" not "most."
5. **One line** — If your hook is longer than one line, cut it

### Hormozi Hook Rewrites:

**IF original is a general introduction:**
```
ORIGINAL: "Hey guys, today I want to talk about building a business..."
REWRITE:  "You can build a $1M business with 3 things:"
```

**IF original is a vague claim:**
```
ORIGINAL: "I've learned a lot about making money over the years..."
REWRITE:  "$0 to $100M in 4 years. Here's what I learned:"
```

**IF original is a conventional take:**
```
ORIGINAL: "I think following your passion is really important..."
REWRITE:  "'Follow your passion' is terrible career advice."
```

## CATEGORY 2: PERSONAL STORY / TIMELINE

### Hormozi's Standard: Stories are compressed. Every line earns its place. Vulnerability IS credibility.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Open at the conflict** — Not "I was born in..." but at the lowest point or turning point
2. **Use timeline format when possible** — Age: Event. Age: Event. Cleanest story structure.
3. **Specific numbers everywhere** — "$100K in debt" not "a lot of debt." "26" not "my mid-twenties."
4. **Show both failure AND success** — The struggle makes the win believable
5. **End with a 2-5 word lesson** — "Never quit." "Do the boring work." "Start before you're ready."
6. **Keep it tight** — If a sentence doesn't build tension or credibility, cut it

### Hormozi Story Structure:
```
[Compressed timeline OR dramatic opening moment]

[The struggle — specific, vulnerable, with numbers]

[The turning point — one sentence]

[The result — specific numbers]

[2-5 word lesson]
```

### Example Transformation:
```
ORIGINAL: "So basically when I was starting out I had nothing, like I was
           working really hard but not making any money and it was really
           tough for a while but then things started to work out..."

REWRITE:  "23: Quit my job with $0 in savings.
           25: Opened a gym. Lost everything.
           26: Slept on the gym floor.
           28: Built it back. Hit $1M.
           31: Sold for $46.2M.

           Every failure was a setup for the next level."
```

## CATEGORY 3: PROBLEM / PAIN CALL-OUT

### Hormozi's Standard: Describe their pain better than they can describe it themselves. They'll assume you have the solution.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Name the SPECIFIC pain** — Not "challenges" but the exact, specific frustration
2. **Use THEIR language** — Write how they talk, not how a consultant talks
3. **Second person** — "You're posting every day..." not "People post every day..."
4. **Make it visual** — Paint the exact scenario they're living
5. **End positioned to escalate** — Set up agitation or solution

### Hormozi Problem Templates:
```
SIMPLE:
"You're [specific situation]. And no matter what you try, [why it persists]."

VISUAL:
"You're posting every day.
Getting 12 likes from your mom and 2 bots.
Wondering why 'content marketing' doesn't work."

WITH GAP:
"You're doing [amount of work].
Getting [underwhelming result].
The gap between effort and outcome? That's the problem."
```

## CATEGORY 4: AGITATION / CONSEQUENCE

### Hormozi's Standard: Use the Value Equation — increase perceived cost of Time Delay and Effort & Sacrifice of NOT acting.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Calculate the cost** — "Every month this continues, you're losing $X"
2. **Comparison pain** — "While you're stuck at [X], your competitors are at [Y]"
3. **Future-pace the bad outcome** — "Imagine 12 months from now, same place"
4. **Stack consequences** — Immediate → near-term → long-term
5. **End with the pivot** — "But it doesn't have to be this way." Then move to solution.

### Hormozi Agitation Language:
```
"Every day you [stay in this situation], [compound consequence]."
"Meanwhile, [comparison group] already [solved this]."
"The real cost isn't [obvious thing]. It's [hidden thing]."
"And this problem compounds. It doesn't get easier. It gets worse."
```

## CATEGORY 5: CONTRARIAN TAKE / REFRAME

### Hormozi's Standard: Take the opposite side of conventional wisdom. Make it feel obvious.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **State the conventional wisdom first** — Then destroy it
2. **One flip per post** — Don't try to be contrarian about five things
3. **Back it with logic or proof** — Contrarian without evidence = edgy. With evidence = insight.
4. **No hedging** — Commit fully. "Is terrible advice." Not "might not be the best advice."
5. **The reframe IS the value** — The reader should think differently after reading one line

### Hormozi Contrarian Templates:
```
SIMPLE FLIP:
"'[Common advice]' is terrible advice.
[Why — in one sentence with proof]."

REFRAME:
"You don't need more [what they think they need].
You need [what they actually need]."

THE REAL ANSWER:
"Everyone's obsessed with [popular thing].
The people actually winning are focused on [unpopular thing]."
```

## CATEGORY 6: TEACHING / FRAMEWORK

### Hormozi's Standard: Name the framework. Explain it simply. Show why it works. This is your Power pillar.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Name it** — Every framework needs a name ("The Value Equation", "The SPCL Framework")
2. **Simplify to math when possible** — Equations > paragraphs
3. **Explain each component in one line**
4. **Show application** — "Here's what this means in practice..."
5. **Specific example with numbers** — Theory + example = understanding
6. **No jargon** — If a teenager can't understand it, simplify it

### Hormozi Framework Template:
```
[Framework name]

[1-2 sentence explanation of what it does]

[Component 1]: [One-line definition]
[Component 2]: [One-line definition]
[Component 3]: [One-line definition]

[How to apply — specific example with numbers]

[One-line insight that makes it memorable]
```

## CATEGORY 7: LIST / ACTIONABLE ADVICE

### Hormozi's Standard: 3-7 items. Parallel structure. Each item = one line. Close with a reframe.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Hook with a number** — "5 things I'd tell my 25-year-old self:"
2. **Parallel structure** — Every item follows the same grammatical pattern
3. **One line per item** — If an item needs two sentences, it's two items
4. **Action verbs** — Start each item with a verb when possible
5. **Closing line ties it together** — Reframe, lesson, or mic drop
6. **Sweet spot is 5 items**

### Hormozi List Template:
```
[Hook with number]:

[Item 1 — short, punchy]
[Item 2 — parallel structure]
[Item 3 — parallel structure]
[Item 4 — parallel structure]
[Item 5 — parallel structure]

[1-line closer that reframes everything above]
```

### Contrast List Template (Hormozi favorite):
```
[Group A]:
- [Behavior 1]
- [Behavior 2]
- [Behavior 3]

[Group B]:
- [Opposite behavior 1]
- [Opposite behavior 2]
- [Opposite behavior 3]

[Insight about the gap]
```

## CATEGORY 8: PROOF / CREDIBILITY

### Hormozi's Standard: Specific numbers or it didn't happen. Lead with the most impressive proof.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Specifics only** — "$46.2M" not "millions." "4,000+ gyms" not "thousands of businesses."
2. **Lead with result, not credential** — "Built a $200M/yr portfolio" not "I'm an investor"
3. **Pair with humility** — SPCL rule: pair Status with Likeness
4. **Third-party validation when available** — Who else says this works?
5. **Make it relatable** — Connect the proof to their potential result

### Hormozi Proof Templates:
```
RESULTS PROOF:
"Our portfolio does $200M+/year. Here's what every winner had in common:"

EXPERIENCE PROOF:
"I spent $70,000/month on content for 3 years. Here's the framework that actually worked:"

PAIRED WITH HUMILITY:
"Slept on a gym floor at 26. Sold for $46.2M at 31. The difference was [one insight]."
```

## CATEGORY 9: TESTIMONIAL / CASE STUDY

### Hormozi's Standard: Specific person, specific numbers, specific timeframe. "One of our clients" + measurable result.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Name or describe them specifically** — "Sarah, a gym owner in Ohio" not "one person"
2. **Before + After with numbers** — "[Person] went from $X to $Y in Z months"
3. **Include the objection they overcame** — "She had no experience and thought it wouldn't work"
4. **Keep their voice** — Don't make it sound corporate
5. **One client, one result** — Don't blend multiple testimonials

### Hormozi Testimonial Template:
```
"[Name/descriptor]: [Before state with numbers]
→ [After state with numbers] in [timeframe].
[One sentence in their voice about the experience]."
```

## CATEGORY 10: SOLUTION / PRODUCT REVEAL

### Hormozi's Standard: State it in one sentence. Then explain the mechanism — what makes it different.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **One sentence reveal** — "That's why I built [name]."
2. **Audience + problem + mechanism** — Who it's for, what it solves, how it works differently
3. **"Without" clause** — "...without [the sacrifice they fear most]"
4. **Value Equation framing** — Which lever does it pull? Faster? Easier? More likely? Bigger outcome?
5. **No jargon** — Plain language. Prospect-level vocabulary.

### Hormozi Solution Template:
```
"That's why I created [Product Name].

[Product Name] helps [specific audience] [achieve specific outcome]
without [biggest sacrifice/fear].

Unlike [alternative], it [specific differentiator].
[Proof element in one sentence]."
```

## CATEGORY 11: DIRECT BENEFIT / OUTCOME

### Hormozi's Standard: Benefits OF benefits. Run the "So what?" test twice on every feature.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Lead with outcome, not mechanism**
2. **"Without" clause** — Every benefit is stronger with what they DON'T sacrifice
3. **Specific beats general** — "Save 3 hours/week" not "save time"
4. **Hit both practical and emotional** — The result AND how they'll feel
5. **Value Equation language** — Faster, easier, more likely, bigger dream outcome

### Hormozi Benefit Templates:
```
SIMPLE: "[Feature] means you [outcome] — without [sacrifice]."

EMOTIONAL: "No more [current pain]. No more [frustration]. Just [desired state]."

STACKED: "You'll [outcome 1]. You'll [outcome 2]. And you'll do it in [timeframe]
without [sacrifice]."
```

## CATEGORY 12: OFFER / VALUE STACK

### Hormozi's Standard: Build a pile of value so large the price looks small. This is the Value Equation in action.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Name every component** — Benefit-focused names, not "Module 3"
2. **Assign dollar values** — Credible standalone value for each piece
3. **Stack deliberately** — Core product → bonuses that remove objections
4. **Total value > price** — The math should make saying no feel irrational
5. **Fast-action bonus** — Something extra for those who act now

---

## CATEGORY 13: PRICE / VALUE / GUARANTEE / URGENCY

### Hormozi's Standard: Never reveal price before maximum value. Transfer ALL risk. Give a REAL reason for urgency.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Value first** — Recap full value before price
2. **Compare strategically** — Cost of problem, cost of alternatives
3. **"Investment" language** — Not "cost" or "price"
4. **Guarantee = unconditional** — No fine print, no conditions
5. **Urgency needs a REASON** — "Limited to 50 because [real reason]" not "Act now!"
6. **Break it down** — Daily/monthly comparison

---

## CATEGORY 14: CALL TO ACTION

### Hormozi's Standard: The value IS the CTA. When he does use one — one ask, non-desperate, action-specific.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Single action only** — Every additional option cuts response
2. **Short** — Under 10 words
3. **Non-desperate** — No begging for follows or shares
4. **Never put links in post body** — First comment or bio only
5. **Match CTA to content type:**
   - Teaching post → "Which one resonates most?"
   - Lead gen → "Comment '[WORD]' and I'll send you [thing]"
   - Story post → [None — the story stands alone]
   - Contrarian → "Agree or disagree?"

---

## CATEGORY 15: MIC DROP / SINGLE TRUTH

### Hormozi's Standard: One idea. Maximum impact. No fluff. The post IS the punchline.

### Rewrite Rules:
1. **Compress to minimum viable words** — If you can cut a word, cut it
2. **No supporting evidence** — The statement must stand alone
3. **Period at the end** — Definitiveness matters
4. **Two structures work:**
   - Single statement: "Do the boring work."
   - Setup + payoff: "Nobody is coming to save you. That's the good news."
5. **No CTA** — Adding one cheapens it
6. **Test: Would Hormozi put this on a black background with white text?** If yes, you nailed it.

### Hormozi Mic Drop Templates:
```
SINGLE TRUTH:
"[Statement]. Period."

SETUP + PAYOFF:
"[Setup that creates expectation].
[Payoff that subverts or deepens it]."

IMPERATIVE:
"Stop [bad thing]. Start [good thing]."

PERMISSION SLIP:
"It's okay to [thing they're afraid to do]."
```
