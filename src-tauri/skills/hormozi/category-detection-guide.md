# Hormozi Category Detection Guide
## How to Classify Every Transcript Segment

Use this file when a transcript segment is ambiguous and you need to identify its primary Hormozi category.

---

## DETECTION METHOD

For each segment, scan for **Primary Signals** (definitive matches) first.
If no Primary Signal is present, look at **Secondary Signals** (contextual clues).
When a segment contains multiple categories, choose by **Dominant Purpose**
(what is this sentence ultimately trying to DO in the content?).

---

## CATEGORY 1: HOOK / OPENER

**What it does in content:** Stops the scroll. Earns the right to be read. Everything fails without this.
**Position:** First line. Period.

**Primary Signals:**
- Positioned at the very start of the transcript
- Bold claim with a specific number ("My company sold for $46.2M")
- Contrarian take that flips conventional wisdom
- "How to stay [bad thing]:" negative flip opener
- Direct address to a specific audience ("If you're in your 20s...")

**Secondary Signals:**
- Under 60 characters — punchy and compressed
- No context, no backstory, no warm-up
- Creates a curiosity gap — "Wait, what?"
- Contains "you" in the first sentence

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"Okay so the thing I want to say is most people are doing this wrong..."
"So basically you can beat like 99 percent of people by just..."
"I want to tell you something that nobody talks about..."
"Here's what I learned after selling my company for 46 million..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PERSONAL STORY: A hook may open with a personal moment, but if it runs 3+ sentences into narrative = PERSONAL STORY
- CONTRARIAN TAKE: If the contrarian idea IS the entire post (not just the opener) = CONTRARIAN TAKE

---

## CATEGORY 2: PERSONAL STORY / TIMELINE

**What it does in content:** Builds credibility + connection. Shows the struggle behind the success.
**Position:** Early — often as or right after the hook.

**Primary Signals:**
- First-person narrative ("When I was...", "I remember...", "Back in...")
- Chronological journey with specific ages, years, or dollar amounts
- Before/after arc: struggle → turning point → result
- References to sleeping on gym floor, being broke, losing everything
- Timeline format (age: event, age: event)

**Secondary Signals:**
- Emotional vulnerability paired with business lesson
- Specific dates, places, dollar amounts
- Self-deprecating opening that builds to credibility
- "Best thing that ever happened to me" reframe

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So when I was 26 I basically lost everything and I was sleeping on the floor..."
"Let me tell you about what happened when I started my first business..."
"I remember being 100k in debt and thinking this is it..."
"The timeline was like 23 quit my job, 25 opened a gym, 26 slept on the floor..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PROOF/CREDIBILITY: Story = narrative journey. Proof = data/results without narrative arc.
- HOOK: A 1-line personal moment used as opener = HOOK. Extended narrative = PERSONAL STORY.

---

## CATEGORY 3: PROBLEM / PAIN CALL-OUT

**What it does in content:** Names the reader's pain so accurately they feel understood. "Pain is the pitch."
**Position:** Early — sets up the value or insight.

**Primary Signals:**
- Describes a specific problem the audience has
- Uses second person ("You're posting every day and getting 12 likes...")
- Names the gap between where they are and where they want to be
- Validates their frustration without wallowing

**Secondary Signals:**
- Language of struggle, frustration, being stuck
- Comparison to what they want vs. what they have
- "Most people" or "If you're like most [audience]" framing
- Ends positioned to escalate (leads into agitation or solution)

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"The number one thing I see is people are doing all the work but getting no results..."
"You're spending all this time on content and getting nothing..."
"Most business owners are stuck at the same revenue for years..."
"If you're posting every day and nobody's buying, here's the problem..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- AGITATION: Problem NAMES the pain. Agitation TWISTS THE KNIFE — consequences, cost, comparison.
- CONTRARIAN TAKE: Problem states what's wrong. Contrarian flips the conventional solution.

---

## CATEGORY 4: AGITATION / CONSEQUENCE

**What it does in content:** Makes staying put MORE uncomfortable than taking action. Hormozi's Value Equation: increase perceived cost of inaction.
**Position:** After problem statement, BEFORE the solution.

**Primary Signals:**
- Stacking consequences ("And what that means is...")
- Future-pacing the bad outcome ("Imagine 5 years from now...")
- Calculating the cost ("Every month you don't fix this, you're losing $X")
- Comparison pain ("While you're stuck, your competitors are...")

**Secondary Signals:**
- Words: costs, losing, missing, falling behind, compounding
- Emotional escalation beyond just naming the problem
- "And it gets worse" or "Here's what nobody tells you" transitions

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"And the real cost isn't just the money it's like your time and energy..."
"Meanwhile everyone else in your space is already doing this..."
"Every day you wait you're basically losing potential customers..."
"And what most people don't realize is this problem compounds..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PROBLEM: States the problem. AGITATION intensifies it.
- ENEMY/VILLAIN: Agitation about consequences vs. blaming an external party.

---

## CATEGORY 5: CONTRARIAN TAKE / REFRAME

**What it does in content:** Flips conventional wisdom. Makes the obvious seem wrong and the counterintuitive seem obvious. Signature Hormozi move.
**Position:** Can be the entire post (micro-post format) or the core insight in a longer piece.

**Primary Signals:**
- Takes the opposite side of conventional advice
- "Everyone says X. They're wrong." structure
- Reframes a negative as a positive (or vice versa)
- "'[Common advice]' is terrible advice" pattern
- Uses contrast/juxtaposition as the primary device

**Secondary Signals:**
- "Here's what nobody talks about"
- "The real [truth/problem/answer] is..."
- Confident, almost confrontational tone
- No hedging or qualifiers

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"Everyone tells you to follow your passion but that's actually terrible advice..."
"The thing people don't understand is hard work doesn't actually build wealth..."
"So motivation is basically a myth, what you really need is systems..."
"People think they need more leads but actually they need a better offer..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PROBLEM: Problem names a struggle. Contrarian FLIPS conventional thinking about it.
- TEACHING/FRAMEWORK: If the contrarian idea leads into a multi-step framework = TEACHING.

---

## CATEGORY 6: TEACHING / FRAMEWORK

**What it does in content:** Delivers actionable value. Frameworks, step-by-step, "here's how." This is Hormozi's Power pillar (SPCL).
**Position:** Body of teaching posts. Often the bulk of longer content.

**Primary Signals:**
- Structured breakdown with steps, pillars, or components
- Named frameworks (Value Equation, SPCL, etc.)
- "Here's the exact playbook" or "Here's how" language
- Numbered or bulleted instructional content
- Explains WHY something works, not just WHAT to do

**Secondary Signals:**
- Teacher/mentor tone — matter-of-fact, no ego
- Math or equations used to simplify concepts
- "Most people do X. Do Y instead." instructional contrast
- Specific tactics with specific numbers

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So the framework I use is basically there's four parts..."
"Here's exactly what you need to do, step one is..."
"The value equation is dream outcome times likelihood divided by..."
"The way I think about it there's three things you need..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- LIST/ADVICE: Framework has a named structure and explains WHY. List just delivers items.
- SOLUTION REVEAL: Solution introduces WHAT you created. Framework teaches HOW something works.

---

## CATEGORY 7: LIST / ACTIONABLE ADVICE

**What it does in content:** Quick-value delivery. Punchy, scannable, immediately useful.
**Position:** Body content — often the entire post.

**Primary Signals:**
- Enumerated or bulleted list of tips, behaviors, or habits
- "X things I'd tell my Y-year-old self" pattern
- Parallel structure across items
- Each item is one line, one sentence

**Secondary Signals:**
- Number in the hook (3 things, 5 ways, 7 habits)
- Items are actionable — reader can do them today
- Closing line that reframes or ties the list together

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So the five things I would say are number one learn to sell..."
"If I had to start over I'd do these three things..."
"The habits that changed everything for me were..."
"Here's what winners do versus what losers do..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- TEACHING/FRAMEWORK: Framework has a named system. List is independent items.
- OFFER: Offer lists product components. Advice lists behaviors or habits.

---

## CATEGORY 8: PROOF / CREDIBILITY

**What it does in content:** Destroys skepticism with evidence. Makes claims believable. Hormozi's Credibility pillar (SPCL).
**Position:** Throughout — heaviest after claims or before asks.

**Primary Signals:**
- Specific numbers and statistics ("$200M+/year portfolio", "3,400 clients")
- Years of experience mentioned with context
- Revenue, profit, or growth numbers tied to claims
- "Our portfolio does...", "I've invested in..."
- Third-party validation (media, publications)

**Secondary Signals:**
- Specific dollar amounts ($46.2M, not "millions")
- Named companies, named clients, named results
- Track record language with measurable outcomes
- Time investment ("After 15 years of testing...")

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"Our portfolio does over 200 million a year in revenue..."
"I've invested in 16 companies and every single winner had this..."
"We've helped over 4000 gyms grow their business..."
"I spent 70 thousand a month on content, here's what I learned..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PERSONAL STORY: Story is narrative journey. Proof is data/results.
- TESTIMONIAL: Proof = your own credentials. Testimonial = someone else's results.

---

## CATEGORY 9: TESTIMONIAL / CASE STUDY

**What it does in content:** Third-party validation. Shows the system works for others, not just the speaker.
**Position:** After teaching content or solution reveal.

**Primary Signals:**
- Speaking about a specific client/customer/student
- "One of my clients/students..." language
- Before/after results from someone else's perspective
- Direct quotes from a third party
- Named individual's specific result with numbers

**Secondary Signals:**
- "Just last [timeframe], [person] told me..."
- "[Person] was skeptical at first..."
- "[Person] went from X to Y in Z timeframe"

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"I had this one client who came in doing like 50k a month..."
"One of our gym owners went from barely surviving to doing 100k..."
"This student of mine, she had no experience and within 6 months..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- PERSONAL STORY: Your own story. TESTIMONIAL = someone else's story.
- PROOF: Aggregate stats = PROOF. One specific person's result = TESTIMONIAL.

---

## CATEGORY 10: SOLUTION / PRODUCT REVEAL

**What it does in content:** Introduces the method, product, or system as the answer. The relief after the pain.
**Position:** After problem/agitation. The pivot moment.

**Primary Signals:**
- "That's why I created/built..." language
- Introducing a named method, program, or approach
- Transitional language from problem → answer
- "Here's what actually works" after describing what doesn't
- Describing the mechanism — HOW it's different

**Secondary Signals:**
- Naming the product or system for the first time
- "Unlike [alternatives], this..."
- The "so I decided to" moment
- Value Equation framing: faster, easier, more likely, bigger outcome

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So what I put together is basically a system that..."
"That's exactly why we built this program for..."
"After years of doing it the wrong way I finally figured out..."
"The solution is actually way simpler than people think..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- DIRECT BENEFIT: Solution = WHAT you created. Benefit = WHAT IT DOES FOR THEM.
- TEACHING: Solution introduces the thing. Teaching explains how things work generally.

---

## CATEGORY 11: DIRECT BENEFIT / OUTCOME

**What it does in content:** Translates features into reader outcomes. "What does this mean for ME?"
**Position:** Woven throughout — after solution reveal, in bullet points, in offer section.

**Primary Signals:**
- Outcome language: "You'll be able to...", "This means you can..."
- "Without having to [sacrifice]" clauses
- Specific result statements with timeframes
- Transformation language (before → after for the READER)
- Second-person focused ("you" as subject)

**Secondary Signals:**
- Ease language ("without [obstacle]", "even if [objection]")
- Specific timeframes ("in 30 days...", "within the first week...")
- Value Equation language: faster, easier, higher likelihood

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"What this means for you is you'll never have to worry about..."
"So basically you'll be able to do this without spending hours on..."
"The result is you walk away with a complete system that..."
"Instead of spending all that time on this stuff you can..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- SOLUTION: The solution is WHAT you created. The benefit is WHAT IT DOES FOR THEM.
- TEACHING: Teaching explains concepts. Benefits state outcomes.

---

## CATEGORY 12: OFFER / VALUE STACK

**What it does in content:** Builds the pile of value. Lists what they get. Makes price seem small.
**Position:** After solution and benefits. Before or alongside price.

**Primary Signals:**
- "You'll get...", "Included is...", "Inside you'll find..."
- Listing components, modules, bonuses, deliverables
- Dollar values assigned to individual components
- "Plus, as a bonus..." reveals
- Stacking value deliberately

**Secondary Signals:**
- Numbered list of components
- Benefit-focused component names
- "Total value: $X" summation

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So what you get when you sign up is access to all the modules..."
"The first thing you get is the complete system plus the bonus..."
"There's three main parts to this, first you get..."
"On top of that we're also throwing in..."
```

---

## CATEGORY 13: PRICE / VALUE / GUARANTEE / URGENCY

**What it does in content:** Justifies the investment, removes risk, creates reason to act NOW.
**Position:** End of sales content. After full value is established.

**Primary Signals:**
- Dollar amounts as price (not revenue claims — those are PROOF)
- Value comparison ("compare that to hiring someone at $X/hour")
- "Money-back guarantee", "refund", "risk-free"
- Limited time, limited spots, deadline language
- "Your investment is..." language

**Secondary Signals:**
- Daily/monthly cost breakdown
- ROI framing ("pays for itself")
- Specific guarantee periods
- Reason WHY the limit exists

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"The investment for the whole program is just..."
"And if it doesn't work out you get a full refund no questions asked..."
"We can only take 50 people in this round because..."
"This pricing goes away after Friday..."
```

---

## CATEGORY 14: CALL TO ACTION

**What it does in content:** Single, clear directive. Converts intent to action.
**Position:** End. Sometimes mid-content for long pieces.

**Primary Signals:**
- Explicit instruction: "Comment", "DM me", "Go to", "Click"
- A URL, keyword, or response mechanism
- "To get started..." language
- Comment-trigger CTAs ("Comment 'OFFERS' and I'll send you...")

**Secondary Signals:**
- Soft engagement: "Agree or disagree?"
- "Follow for more" (rare)
- Link-in-bio references
- NEVER puts links in post body (Hormozi rule)

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"So if you want the framework just comment offers below..."
"Go to acquisition dot com and grab the free book..."
"DM me the word scale and I'll send you the playbook..."
"Just hit follow if you want more of this kind of stuff..."
```

---

## CATEGORY 15: MIC DROP / SINGLE TRUTH

**What it does in content:** Maximum impact, minimum words. One idea. No fluff. The post IS the point.
**Position:** The entire post — or the closing line.

**Primary Signals:**
- 1-3 sentences total — the whole piece
- A single powerful statement or reframe
- No supporting evidence or explanation
- Confident, definitive, period at the end
- "Do the boring work." energy

**Secondary Signals:**
- Two-line setup + payoff structure
- Imperative commands ("Stop making excuses. Start making offers.")
- Permission slips ("It's okay to outgrow people.")
- No CTA needed — the statement IS the value

**Common Raw Transcript Patterns:**
```
"The thing I keep coming back to is nobody is coming to save you..."
"At the end of the day it's just do the boring work..."
"You don't become confident by shouting affirmations you become confident by stacking proof..."
"Your excuses are just fears with better marketing that's it..."
```

**Distinguish from:**
- HOOK: If it's the opening line leading to more content = HOOK. If it's the ENTIRE piece = MIC DROP.
- CONTRARIAN TAKE: If it flips conventional wisdom = CONTRARIAN. If it's a standalone declaration = MIC DROP.
