## 1. Hook Philosophy

Hormozi's hooks follow three principles:

**Principle 1: Short kills.**
Most of his hooks are under 60 characters. Some are under 30. The hook's ONLY job is to make someone stop scrolling and click "See more."

**Principle 2: Lead with the unexpected.**
Either a bold claim, a surprising number, or a contrarian take. Never start with context, backstory, or "I've been thinking about..."

**Principle 3: Create a curiosity gap.**
The hook should make the reader think "Wait, what?" or "How?" or "That can't be right." Then the body delivers.

**What Hormozi NEVER uses as a hook:**
- "Last week I was thinking about..." (boring setup)
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (cliché)
- "I'm excited to announce..." (corporate)
- "Happy [day of week]!" (filler)
- Generic greetings or context-setting

## 2. Hook Categories

### Category A: The Number Hook
Uses a specific, attention-grabbing number to establish credibility or create curiosity.

1. "My company sold for $46,200,000"
2. "You can beat 99% of people by:"
3. "I went from $0 to $100M in 4 years. Here's what I learned:"
4. "She has 5,800 followers. Her average post gets 19 likes."
5. "3 things that changed my business forever:"
6. "I spent $70,000/month on content. Here's the exact framework:"
7. "You only need 3 things to win:"
8. "$0 to $1M took me 3 years. $1M to $10M took 18 months. Here's why:"
9. "I've invested in 16 companies. Every winner had this in common:"
10. "85% of businesses fail. The 15% that survive all do this:"

### Category B: The Contrarian Hook
Takes conventional wisdom and flips it upside down.

11. "The biggest risk to your future isn't your competition."
12. "'Follow your passion' is terrible career advice."
13. "The best marketing strategy? Stop marketing."
14. "You don't need more leads. You need a better offer."
15. "Hard work doesn't build wealth. Leverage does."
16. "Your boss isn't the problem. Your skill set is."
17. "Motivation is a myth. Systems are everything."
18. "The best investment I ever made wasn't in stocks."
19. "Hustle culture is a trap. Here's what works instead:"
20. "Networking is overrated. Here's what's underrated:"

### Category C: The "How to Stay Poor" (Negative Flip)
Tells people what NOT to do — but in a way that makes them realize they're doing it.

21. "How to stay poor:"
22. "How to guarantee your business fails:"
23. "How to make sure nobody buys from you:"
24. "5 ways to waste your 20s:"
25. "The fastest way to lose everything:"
26. "If you want to stay average, keep doing this:"
27. "How to make sure you never get promoted:"
28. "Want to stay broke? Follow this advice:"

### Category D: The Personal Story Hook
Opens with a vulnerable or dramatic personal moment.

29. "My first business failed."
30. "I was $100K in debt."
31. "Throwback from seven years ago."
32. "I almost quit."
33. "Nobody believed in me. Including me."
34. "I got fired at 23. Best thing that ever happened."
35. "I slept on the gym floor for 6 months."
36. "My wife and I had $1,000 left in our account."

### Category E: The Bold Declaration
A statement so confident it demands attention.

37. "Nobody is coming to save you."
38. "You're one offer away from changing your life."
39. "Most people will never be rich. Here's why:"
40. "The person you want to become is on the other side of the work you keep avoiding."
41. "Discipline is choosing between what you want now and what you want most."
42. "I cannot lose if I do not quit."
43. "Your excuses are just fears with better marketing."
44. "The market doesn't care about your feelings."

### Category F: The "Imagine" / Thought Experiment Hook
Invites the reader into a scenario.

45. "Imagine you're talking to God about the person you want to become."
46. "Imagine you could only work 2 hours a day. What would you do?"
47. "Picture yourself 5 years from now — did you take the risk or play it safe?"
48. "What if everything you're afraid of already happened?"

### Category G: The Direct Address Hook
Speaks to a specific audience by name.

49. "A message for friends of entrepreneurs:"
50. "To every entrepreneur who feels behind:"
51. "If you're in your 20s and feel lost, read this:"
52. "Dear business owner who's about to give up:"
53. "This is for the person who 'doesn't have time':"

### Category H: The Question Hook
Asks something that makes the reader think.

54. "What would you do if you couldn't fail?"
55. "Why do poor people give rich people money advice?"
56. "When's the last time you did something uncomfortable?"
57. "What if your stress isn't a problem?"
58. "How long will you wait before you demand the best for yourself?"

## 3. CTA Philosophy

Hormozi's approach to CTAs is radically simple: **the value IS the CTA.** If the post is good enough, people will follow, comment, and share without being asked.

When he does use a CTA, it's:
- Short (under 10 words)
- Single ask (never multiple CTAs)
- Non-desperate (no begging)
- Action-specific (tells them exactly what to do)

**His rule:** External links in the post body kill reach. If linking, put it in the first comment or bio.

## 4. CTA Templates by Type

### Engagement CTAs (most common)
```
Agree or disagree?
Which one resonates most?
What would you add?
Tag someone who needs this.
```

### Lead Generation CTAs
```
Comment "OFFERS" and I'll send you the framework.
Comment "[WORD]" below and I'll DM you the guide.
DM me "SCALE" for the playbook.
```

### Subtle CTAs (embedded in content)
```
[No explicit CTA — the post just delivers value and trusts the reader to take action]
```

### Profile/Follow CTAs (rare)
```
Follow for more.
Follow along for the journey.
```

### Link CTAs (always in first comment, never in post body)
```
Link in first comment.
Full breakdown in my bio.
Grab the free book at acquisition.com
```

## 5. Hook + CTA Combinations

**For teaching posts:**
Hook: "3 things I wish I knew before starting a business:"
CTA: "Which one hits hardest?"

**For story posts:**
Hook: "My first business failed."
CTA: [None — the story stands alone]

**For contrarian posts:**
Hook: "'Follow your passion' is terrible career advice."
CTA: "Agree or disagree?"

**For lead gen posts:**
Hook: "I spent 10 years learning to write offers. Here's the cheat code:"
CTA: "Comment 'OFFERS' and I'll send you the full framework."

**For list posts:**
Hook: "How to stay poor:"
CTA: "Tag someone who needs a wake-up call."

---

**Usage note:** Mix and match these templates. The magic is in combining a strong hook category with your specific topic and authentic voice. Always deliver on the hook's promise in the body — Hormozi never uses clickbait without payoff.
