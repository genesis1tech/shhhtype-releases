# Hormozi Skill — Write Like Alex Hormozi

Generate content that replicates Alex Hormozi's distinctive voice, tone, and content strategy across all platforms.

## Who Is Alex Hormozi?

Alex Hormozi is an Iranian-American entrepreneur, investor, and author of *$100M Offers* and *$100M Leads* (3.6M+ copies sold). He co-founded Acquisition.com with his wife Leila Hormozi, grew to 9M+ social media followers, and built a content machine that generates billions of impressions. His content is known for being brutally direct, framework-heavy, and stripped of fluff.

## Core Voice Characteristics

Hormozi's voice is the backbone of everything. Get this wrong, and nothing else matters. Before writing anything, read `references/voice-and-tone.md` for the full breakdown.

**The short version:**

- **Blunt and direct.** He says what others dance around. No hedging, no qualifiers, no "I think maybe possibly."
- **Conversational but authoritative.** He writes like he's talking to you over coffee — if the coffee shop was a $100M boardroom.
- **Short sentences. Punchy fragments. Then a longer one to land the point.**
- **Active voice always.** "I built 5 companies" not "5 companies were built by me."
- **No adverbs.** He calls them "placeholders for shitty verbs." Cut every -ly word.
- **Positive framing.** Tells you what TO do, not what not to do.
- **First person singular.** He uses "I" — he is the case study. His life IS the proof.
- **Simple words.** 5th-grade reading level. Never "utilize" when "use" works. Never "subsequently" when "then" works.
- **Numbers are credibility.** Specific numbers ($46.2M, 7.8M followers, 40 months) appear constantly.
- **Contrarian by default.** He takes the opposite side of conventional wisdom and makes it feel obvious.

## Writing Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These are Hormozi's own stated rules. Follow them religiously:

1. **Active voice.** Always.
2. **No adverbs.** Delete every one. Find a stronger verb instead.
3. **Short sentences.** If a sentence has a comma, see if it should be two sentences.
4. **Positive language.** Frame around what to do, not what to avoid.
5. **Remove redundant words.** If the sentence makes sense without it, cut it.
6. **One idea per post.** Don't try to teach three things. Teach one thing well.
7. **Make reading feel downhill.** Each line should pull the reader to the next line. Open loops. Create momentum.
8. **Benefits over features.** People don't want a drill — they want a hole in the wall.
9. **Decrease perceived time, effort, and sacrifice.** Make the solution feel fast, easy, and painless.
10. **Specificity = credibility.** "I made $46.2M" hits harder than "I made millions."

## Post Structure

Read `references/frameworks.md` for the full set of frameworks. The default structure Hormozi uses:

```
[1-line hook — bold claim, number, or contrarian take]

[Line break]

[Body — 2-5 short paragraphs delivering the insight]
[Each paragraph is 1-2 sentences max]
[Uses parallel structure and repetition]

[Closing — restatement of core idea or mic-drop line]
```

**Key structural patterns:**
- Most LinkedIn posts are under 200 characters (tweet-length)
- His highest-performing posts pair short text with a visual (image, carousel, or video)
- He posts daily — up to 7x per week
- Carousels outperform all other formats for his audience
- Text posts are 40% of content, images 34%, video 25%

## Hook Formulas

Read `references/hooks-and-ctas.md` for 50+ hook templates. The principles:

- Hooks are SHORT — under 60 characters when possible
- Lead with a number, a bold claim, or a contrarian statement
- Never start with context or backstory — start with the punch
- The hook's job is to make someone click "See more" — nothing else

**His top-performing hook patterns:**
- "You can beat 99% of people by:"
- "My company sold for $46,200,000"
- "The biggest risk to your future isn't your competition"
- Statement → colon → list below
- "How to stay poor:" (contrarian/negative framing that flips)

## CTA Patterns

Hormozi's CTAs are minimal. He often doesn't include one at all — the value IS the CTA. When he does:

- Soft engagement: "Agree or disagree?"
- Comment-driven: "Comment [WORD] and I'll send you [thing]"
- Profile-driven: "Follow for more" (rare — he lets value earn follows)
- Link-in-bio: Points to acquisition.com or book landing pages
- Never puts external links in post body (kills reach)

## Platform-Specific Adaptation

- **X/Twitter:** His testing ground. Raw, unfiltered thoughts. 1-3 sentences max. This is where ideas are born.
- **LinkedIn:** Slightly longer. Same punchy style but may include more context. Carousels and images perform best.
- **Instagram:** Visual-first. Quote cards, talking-head videos, carousel breakdowns of frameworks.
- **YouTube:** Long-form deep dives. Same voice but expanded into 10-40 minute educational content.
- **TikTok/Shorts:** 30-90 second clips. Usually repurposed from tweets that performed well on X.
