## 1. The Value Equation

Hormozi's most famous framework from *$100M Offers*. Use this when creating content about offers, pricing, or value delivery.

```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

**To increase value, you either:**
- Increase the dream outcome (make the result bigger)
- Increase perceived likelihood (make them believe it'll work)
- Decrease time delay (make it faster)
- Decrease effort & sacrifice (make it easier)

**Content application:** When writing about any product, service, or strategy — frame it through the Value Equation. Which lever are you pulling? Make that explicit.

**Example post using this framework:**
```
People don't pay for your product.
They pay for the gap between where they are and where they want to be.

Want to charge more? Close the gap faster.

Dream Outcome × Likelihood of Achievement
÷ Time Delay × Effort Required

Increase the top. Decrease the bottom.
That's the whole game.
```

## 2. The SPCL Framework

From Hormozi's content strategy. The 4 pillars of building influence:

- **Status** — What you have that others want (expertise, results, access, lifestyle)
- **Power** — Say-do consistency. Your advice actually gets results.
- **Credibility** — Proof you've done the thing. Receipts.
- **Likeness** — People trust people like them. Relatability factor.

**Content application:** Every piece of content should lean on at least one SPCL pillar.

| Pillar | Content Type | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Status | Flex posts, milestones | "My company sold for $46.2M" |
| Power | Teaching frameworks | "Here's the exact playbook I used:" |
| Credibility | Case studies, proof | "Our portfolio does $200M+/year" |
| Likeness | Vulnerability, humor | "I slept on the gym floor" |

**Rule:** Never lead with Status alone — it comes off as bragging. Pair it with Likeness (vulnerability) or Power (teaching).

## 3. Pain-is-the-Pitch

One of Hormozi's core copywriting principles: Instead of telling people what to do, describe their pain so accurately that they sell themselves on the solution.

**The principle:** When you describe someone's problem better than they can describe it themselves, they automatically assume you have the solution.

**Content application:**
```
Step 1: Name the specific pain (not generic — specific)
Step 2: Agitate it — show consequences of inaction
Step 3: Present the shift (not the full solution — just the key insight)
Step 4: Show proof it works (your numbers, your story)
```

**Example:**
```
You're posting every day.
Getting 12 likes from your mom and 2 bots.
Wondering why "content marketing" doesn't work.

Here's the problem:
You're creating content about what YOU want to talk about.
Not what your audience needs to hear.

Flip the script.
Stop teaching. Start solving.

When I made this one shift, I went from 0 to 1M followers in 6 months.
```

## 4. The Contrast Framework

Hormozi's most-used structural device. Put two opposites side by side. Let the reader see themselves in one.

**Pattern:**
```
[Successful behavior] vs. [Unsuccessful behavior]
[What winners do] vs. [What losers do]
[Rich habits] vs. [Poor habits]
```

**Example:**
```
Successful people make decisions quickly and change them slowly.
Unsuccessful people make decisions slowly and change them quickly.
```

**Example (list format):**
```
Rich people:
- Buy assets
- Invest in themselves
- Say no to almost everything

Poor people:
- Buy liabilities
- Save pennies on coffee
- Say yes to everything
```

**Why it works:** People self-sort. Readers place themselves in one category and either feel validated or motivated to change. Either way — engagement.

## 5. The Timeline Framework

Compresses a multi-year journey into a punchy, scannable list. Hormozi's highest-engagement format.

**Pattern:**
```
[Age/Year]: [Event — ideally with specific number]
[Age/Year]: [Next event]
[Age/Year]: [Breakthrough]
...
[1-line lesson]
```

**Rules:**
- Keep each line to one event
- Include specific numbers where possible
- Show BOTH failures and successes
- End with a short, powerful lesson (2-5 words)
- The trajectory should show struggle → growth → result

**Example:**
```
22: Graduated with a degree I'd never use
23: Quit my 9-5 — started consulting
24: Made $42K — thought I made it
25: Lost my biggest client — went to $0
26: Built first product — $156K first year
28: Hired first team — crossed $1M
30: Sold the company — started over

Every "failure" was a setup for the next level.
```

## 6. The "How to Stay [Bad Thing]" Framework

A Hormozi signature move. Instead of telling people what to do, tell them how to guarantee the bad outcome. The reader recognizes themselves and self-corrects.

**Pattern:**
```
How to [stay poor / fail / lose everything / waste your potential]:

1. [Bad behavior presented as advice]
2. [Another bad behavior]
3. [Continue...]
...
[Optional closer: "Do the opposite."]
```

**Example:**
```
How to guarantee your startup fails:

1. Spend 6 months on a logo
2. Build a product nobody asked for
3. Hire your friends
4. Avoid talking to customers
5. Raise money before making money
6. Pivot every 2 weeks
7. Blame the market

Do the opposite and you have a shot.
```

**Why it works:** Negative framing grabs attention (threat response). But the REAL message is positive — do the opposite.

## 7. The List-Drop Framework

A quick-value post that delivers actionable insights in list format.

**Pattern:**
```
[Hook with number]

[Item 1 — short, punchy]
[Item 2]
[Item 3]
...
[Closing line that ties it together]
```

**Rules:**
- 3-7 items (sweet spot is 5)
- Each item is one line, one sentence
- Use parallel structure
- No numbering needed — bullets or line breaks work
- Closing line reframes or adds insight

**Example:**
```
5 things I'd tell my 25-year-old self:

Learn to sell before you learn to build.
Your network is your net worth (cliché because it's true).
Speed > perfection. Ship it.
The people around you determine your ceiling.
Start the thing you keep "planning to start."

You don't need more time. You need more courage.
```

## 8. The Single Truth Framework

Hormozi's "mic drop" format. One idea. No fluff. Maximum impact.

**Pattern:**
```
[Statement 1 — sets up expectation]

[Statement 2 — subverts or reframes it]
```

Or simply:
```
[One powerful statement. Period.]
```

**Examples:**
```
Nobody is coming to save you.
That's the good news.
```

```
People pay for speed.
The faster you solve their problem, the more you can charge.
Period.
```

```
Do the boring work.
```

## 9. The Content Repurposing Pipeline

Hormozi's actual content production system:

```
Step 1: Tweet raw ideas on X/Twitter
Step 2: Measure which tweets perform (engagement, impressions)
Step 3: Take winning tweets → expand into short-form video scripts
Step 4: Record 30-90 second videos
Step 5: Post videos across TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts
Step 6: Take best-performing shorts → expand into long-form YouTube
Step 7: Pull quotes from long-form → create carousel posts for LinkedIn
Step 8: Newsletter and podcast repurpose the best of everything
```

**The key insight:** He tests ideas cheaply (tweets cost nothing) before investing production resources. By the time content reaches YouTube, it's already proven to resonate.

## 10. Post Structure Templates

### Template A: The Quick Hit (LinkedIn/X)
```
[Bold 1-line hook]

[2-3 sentence insight]

[1-line closer or mic drop]
```
Best for: Daily posts, thought leadership, hot takes

### Template B: The List Post (LinkedIn/X)
```
[Hook with number]:

- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]
- [Point 4]
- [Point 5]

[Reframe/lesson in 1 line]
```
Best for: Actionable tips, frameworks, contrarian lists

### Template C: The Story Post (LinkedIn)
```
[Dramatic opening — moment of struggle]

[Brief context — 2 sentences max]

[The shift — what changed]

[The result — specific numbers]

[The lesson — 1 sentence]
```
Best for: Personal brand building, credibility, engagement

### Template D: The Contrast Post (any platform)
```
[Group A] does [behavior A].
[Group B] does [behavior B].

[Insight about why this matters]
```
Best for: Engagement bait, shareability, hot takes

### Template E: The Framework Post (LinkedIn/YouTube)
```
[Name the framework]

[Explain it in 1-2 sentences]

[Break down each component — 1 line each]

[Show how it applies — example with numbers]

[CTA or closing thought]
```
Best for: Teaching content, carousel posts, long-form
