## 1. Top LinkedIn Posts

### Post #1 — "The Timeline" (18,400+ likes — his highest performer)
```
23: Quit my consulting job
25: Opened first gym — lost everything
26: Slept on the gym floor
27: Turned first gym around
28: Launched Gym Launch — helped 4,000+ gyms
29: Started Prestige Labs — $1.7M first month
30: Launched ALAN — scaled to $25M/year
31: Sold 66% of my companies for $46.2M
32: Started Acquisition.com — current portfolio does $200M+/year

Never quit.
```
**WHY IT WORKS:**
- Hook is his personal journey — chronological timeline is irresistible
- Every line has a specific number (age, dollar amount, metric)
- Shows struggle AND success — relatable vulnerability
- Simple black-and-white photo accompanies it
- The closing "Never quit" is a 2-word mic drop
- People comment to congratulate and share their own timelines

### Post #2 — "Beat 99%" (14,000+ likes)
```
You can beat 99% of people by:

- Not drinking
- Working out daily
- Reading 30 min/day
- Sleeping 8 hours
- Having a bias for action over talk

None of these are hard.
All of them are boring.
That's why they work.
```
**WHY IT WORKS:**
- Hook uses "99%" — specific, aspirational number
- List is deceptively simple — each item is accessible
- The twist: "None of these are hard. All of them are boring."
- Reframes success as discipline, not talent
- Parallel structure in closing three lines

### Post #3 — "The Biggest Risk" (12,000+ likes)
```
The biggest risk to your future isn't your competition.

It's the comfortable life you're living right now.
```
**WHY IT WORKS:**
- 2 sentences. That's it. Under 100 characters total.
- Contrarian reframe — you expect "competition" but get "comfort"
- Feels like a slap and a hug at the same time
- Extremely shareable — works as a quote card

### Post #4 — "First Business Failed"
```
My first business failed.

My second business almost killed me.

My third made me a millionaire.

Most people quit after the first.
```
**WHY IT WORKS:**
- Progressive story in 4 lines
- Each line escalates (failed → almost killed → millionaire)
- The punchline reframes the entire post about quitting
- Extremely short but emotionally complete

### Post #5 — "Message for Friends of Entrepreneurs"
```
A message for friends of entrepreneurs:

Your friend isn't ignoring you.
They're building something.
They're scared.
They're tired.
They're betting everything.

The best thing you can say:
"I believe in you."
```
**WHY IT WORKS:**
- Speaks TO a secondary audience (friends, not entrepreneurs)
- Parallel repetition ("They're...")
- Emotional vulnerability rare for his brand — stands out
- Universal relatability — everyone knows an entrepreneur

## 4. Story-Format Posts

Hormozi tells stories in compressed, punchy format. No flowery prose. No setup paragraphs.

**Pattern:**
```
[Moment of struggle/failure]
[Brief context — 1-2 sentences]
[What I did differently]
[The result — specific numbers]
[The lesson — one line]
```

**Example:**
```
I was $100K in debt.

Living in a gym. Showering at the YMCA.
Eating protein bars for dinner.

I made one decision that changed everything:
I stopped selling what I wanted to sell
and started selling what people wanted to buy.

6 months later: $1M in revenue.
18 months later: $25M.

The product didn't change. The offer did.
```

## 5. Framework/Teaching Posts

When Hormozi teaches, he reduces complex ideas to simple equations or numbered steps.

**The Value Equation (his most famous framework):**
```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)

Want to charge more?
→ Increase dream outcome
→ Increase perceived likelihood
→ Decrease time delay
→ Decrease effort & sacrifice

That's it. That's the whole game.
```

**The SPCL Framework:**
```
4 things that build influence:

Status — What you have that others want
Power — Say-do consistency (your advice gets results)
Credibility — Proof you've done the thing
Likeness — People trust people like them

Master all 4 and you become impossible to ignore.
```
