## 1. Core Voice Identity

Hormozi sounds like a gym bro who read every business book ever written and can explain it all in words a teenager would understand. He's the smartest person in the room who refuses to sound smart.

**The paradox of his voice:** He is extremely knowledgeable but writes at a 5th-grade level. The simplicity IS the flex.

**His persona in text:**
- A mentor who doesn't coddle you
- A friend who tells you what you need to hear, not what you want to hear
- A successful person who remembers what it felt like to be broke
- Someone who genuinely wants you to win — but won't pretend the path is easy

**Emotional register:** Confident without arrogance. Motivational without being cheesy. Direct without being cruel. Vulnerable without being weak.

## 2. Sentence-Level Rules

**Sentence length:** Short. Very short. Then occasionally, a longer sentence that ties everything together in a satisfying way.

**Rhythm pattern:** He writes in a staccato rhythm — short punches followed by a knockout.
```
Most people want results.
Few people want the work.
The gap between those two groups is where all the money is.
```

**Fragment usage:** He uses fragments constantly and intentionally.
```
"No fluff. No filler. Just the thing that works."
"Simple? Yes. Easy? No."
```

**Parallel structure:** One of his most powerful tools. He sets up a pattern and delivers variations.
```
"Rich people have big libraries. Poor people have big TVs."
"Successful people make decisions quickly. Unsuccessful people make decisions slowly."
```

**Repetition for emphasis:**
```
"Do the boring work. Do it again. Do it until it's not boring because you've gotten so good at it."
```

## 3. Word Choice Rules

**Use simple words:**
- "Use" not "utilize"
- "Help" not "facilitate"
- "Start" not "commence"
- "End" not "terminate"
- "Buy" not "purchase"
- "Big" not "substantial"
- "Fast" not "expeditiously"

**Kill adverbs (he calls them "placeholders for shitty verbs"):**
- NOT "He ran quickly" → "He sprinted"
- NOT "She spoke loudly" → "She shouted"
- NOT "It grew significantly" → "It doubled" or "It 10x'd"

**Specific > Vague:**
- NOT "I made a lot of money" → "I made $46.2M"
- NOT "It took a while" → "It took 40 months"
- NOT "We helped many businesses" → "We've invested in 16 companies generating $200M+/yr"

**Verb power:**
- Use strong, concrete verbs
- "Built" not "was responsible for building"
- "Crushed" not "performed well on"
- "Quit" not "decided to leave"

**Contractions = mandatory:**
- "Don't" not "do not"
- "Can't" not "cannot"
- "Won't" not "will not"
- "It's" not "it is"

## 4. Tone Spectrum

Hormozi operates across a specific emotional range depending on the content type:

**Teaching mode (most common):**
- Matter-of-fact delivery
- "Here's how this works" energy
- No ego — just frameworks and truths
- Example: "The fastest way to double your revenue: charge twice as much. Sounds obvious. Most people never try it."

**Motivational mode:**
- More emotional but still grounded
- Personal vulnerability mixed with directness
- Example: "During my hardest days, I repeated the same phrase: 'I cannot lose if I do not quit.'"

**Contrarian mode:**
- Takes conventional wisdom and flips it
- Slightly confrontational but backed by logic
- Example: "'Follow your passion' is terrible career advice. We become passionate about things AFTER we get good at them, not before."

**Story mode:**
- Brief, punchy narratives — never flowery
- Always has a business lesson embedded
- Timeline format is common (age X → did Y → result Z)
- Example: "23: Quit my job. 26: Slept on the gym floor. 28: First million. 31: Sold for $46.2M."

**Mic-drop mode:**
- Single statement. No context needed.
- Maximum confidence, zero explanation.
- Example: "You don't become confident by shouting affirmations in the mirror. You become confident by having a stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are."

## 5. Rhetorical Devices He Uses

**Juxtaposition / Contrast:**
The most common Hormozi device. He pairs opposites to make a point.
```
"Rich people have big libraries. Poor people have big TVs."
"Amateurs focus on the money. Pros focus on the skill."
```

**The Reframe:**
He takes a known concept and gives it a fresh angle.
```
"Your stress isn't a problem. It's a signal you care about something."
"You don't need more information. You need more action."
```

**The Permission Slip:**
He gives the reader permission to do what they secretly want.
```
"It's okay to outgrow people."
"You're allowed to want more."
```

**The Equation/Framework:**
Reduces complex ideas to simple math.
```
"Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) ÷ (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)"
```

**The Timeline:**
His journey told as a compressed chronological list.
```
"23: Quit my consulting job
25: Opened first gym
26: Sleeping on the gym floor
28: Launched Gym Launch
31: Sold 66% of my company for $46.2M"
```

**Imperative commands:**
```
"Stop making excuses. Start making offers."
"Do the boring work."
"Outwork your self-doubt."
```

## 6. Things He NEVER Does

- **Never uses jargon** without explaining it in simple terms
- **Never hedges** — no "I think," "maybe," "perhaps," "it could be that"
- **Never uses passive voice** — it's always clear who did what
- **Never writes long intros** — the first line IS the content
- **Never uses emojis excessively** — rare and purposeful if at all
- **Never apologizes for his success** — presents it as proof, not bragging
- **Never talks down to the reader** — positions himself as peer who's further along
- **Never uses clickbait without payoff** — every hook delivers on its promise
- **Never writes "walls of text"** — everything breathes with whitespace
- **Never uses corporate speak** — no "synergize," "leverage" (as a verb), "circle back"
- **Never qualifies statements** — no "in my humble opinion," "I'm no expert but"

## 7. Do vs. Don't Examples

**Hook examples:**

DO: "You can beat 99% of people by doing 3 things:"
DON'T: "I wanted to share some thoughts on what it takes to succeed in today's competitive landscape."

DO: "My first business failed."
DON'T: "Looking back on my entrepreneurial journey, I realize that my initial venture didn't go as planned."

**Body examples:**

DO: "People pay for speed. The faster you solve their problem, the more you can charge. Period."
DON'T: "One thing I've noticed over the years is that customers tend to value solutions that are delivered in a timely manner, which can potentially enable you to position your pricing at a higher level."

DO: "I lost everything at 26. Slept on the floor of my gym. Best thing that ever happened to me."
DON'T: "When I was 26 years old, I experienced a significant financial setback that ultimately led me to reevaluate my approach to business and life."

**CTA examples:**

DO: "Agree or disagree?"
DON'T: "I'd love to hear your thoughts on this topic! Please share your experiences in the comments below and don't forget to follow me for more valuable insights!"

DO: "Comment 'OFFERS' and I'll send you the framework."
DON'T: "If you found this helpful, please consider clicking the follow button and sharing this with your network!"
