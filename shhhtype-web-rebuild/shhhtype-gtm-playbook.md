## Part 1: Core Messaging Framework

### The One-Liner

> **ShhhType turns your voice into ready-to-publish content — LinkedIn posts, DMs, and more — with a single spoken command.**

Use this as the default description everywhere: bio, website hero, Product Hunt tagline, elevator pitch.

### Positioning Statement (Internal)

For LinkedIn creators and B2B sales professionals who need to produce content and personalized messages daily, ShhhType is a voice-to-content tool that transforms raw speech into platform-ready output through voice-triggered AI skills. Unlike dictation apps that just transcribe, ShhhType structures, formats, and optimizes your words for the platform you're publishing on — without you ever touching the keyboard to edit.

### Value Proposition Hierarchy

| Level | Statement | Use Where |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | Speak your ideas. Publish them instantly. | Hero headline, ads, first impression |
| **Secondary** | Voice-triggered skills turn your speech into LinkedIn posts, DMs, and connection notes — formatted, structured, and ready to go. | Feature pages, explainer content |
| **Tertiary** | Dual-engine transcription (offline + cloud), multi-segment composition buffer, and an extensible skill system you can customize for any workflow. | Technical audiences, comparison pages |

### Three Pillars of Messaging

Every piece of ShhhType content should ladder up to one of these three pillars:

**Pillar 1: Speed**
"What used to take 30 minutes now takes 30 seconds."
The ICP's most visceral pain is time. They know what to say. The bottleneck is the gap between thinking and publishing. ShhhType collapses that gap.

**Pillar 2: Authenticity**
"Your voice. Your ideas. Just structured better."
The ICP's second pain is that AI tools don't sound like them. ShhhType uses THEIR spoken words as input. The AI restructures — it doesn't replace. This is the key differentiation from ChatGPT and generic AI writers.

**Pillar 3: Consistency**
"Post every day without it consuming your morning."
The ICP knows consistency wins on LinkedIn. But consistency requires low friction. When creating a post takes 60 seconds instead of 30 minutes, posting daily becomes trivially easy.

## Part 6: Messaging Don'ts

These are traps. Avoid them.

**Don't say "AI-powered dictation tool."**
This puts ShhhType in the dictation category and invites comparison to WisprFlow ($12/mo) and free alternatives. Frame as "voice-to-content" to justify the $20 price point and differentiate.

**Don't say "write posts for you."**
This implies fabrication. The ICP is terrified of sounding like AI. Always say "structures your words" or "formats your ideas" — never "writes for you."

**Don't lead with features.**
"Dual transcription engine with Metal GPU acceleration" means nothing to the ICP. Lead with the outcome: "Speak for 30 seconds, get a LinkedIn post." Features are for the comparison page, not the hook.

**Don't compare to WisprFlow head-on.**
WisprFlow has brand awareness and polish. Comparing directly elevates them. Instead, compare to the ICP's CURRENT workflow: "vs. typing it yourself," "vs. ChatGPT," "vs. your ghostwriter." Let ShhhType be a new category, not a cheaper alternative.

**Don't say "works in any app" as the headline.**
This is a dictation-tool selling point. It's a feature, not a message. Mention it in the body ("works anywhere on your Mac or Windows") but never lead with it.

**Don't use "Shhh" puns excessively.**
One or two is fine. A whole campaign of "Shhh, let your voice do the talking" gets old fast and trivializes the product.
